Business Overview: Teradata Corporation is a leading provider of multi-cloud data warehouse platforms, helping companies leverage their data for real-time intelligence. The platform integrates and simplifies multi-cloud data and analytics ecosystems, streamlines data access and management, and enables businesses to gain value from diverse data types. The company is undergoing a business transformation and realigning and streamlining operations to become a cloud-first growth company. This includes workforce reductions, a voluntary separation program, and transitioning employees to virtual or co-working environments. Key priorities include expanding the Teradata Vantage platform, expanding footprint with existing customers, increasing focus on diversity and inclusiveness, and driving operational excellence. Metrics used to track progress include Annual Recurring Revenue (ARR), Public Cloud ARR, and Remaining Performance Obligation/Backlog.

Results of Operations: in july 2019, the financial accounting standards board ("fasb") issued accounting standards update 2019-07, "codification updates to sec sections-amendments to sec paragraphs pursuant to sec final rule releases no. 33-10532, disclosure update and simplification", which makes a number of changes meant to simplify certain disclosures in financial condition and results of operations, particularly by eliminating year-to-year comparisons between prior periods previously disclosed. in accordance with the relevant aspects of the rule covering the current year annual report, we now include disclosures on results of operations for fiscal year 2020 versus 2019 only. for discussion of fiscal year 2019 versus 2018 see "item 7. management’s discussion and analysis of financial condition and results of operations" in our annual report filed with the sec for the fiscal year ended december 31, 2019.

Revenues: - total revenue decreased by $63 million, or 3%, in 2020. recurring revenue grew 7%, driven by transition to subscription-based transactions from perpetual software licenses and hardware transactions. public cloud arr increased by 165%, and arr growth was 11% higher than expected. total revenue is expected to grow low-single-digit percentage in 2021 compared to 2020. Revenues from perpetual software licenses and hardware decreased 31%, and consulting services revenue decreased 28%. Currency translation is expected to have 2 percentage points positive impact on full-year 2021 total revenues. Subscription and public cloud-related arr was $960 million, up 37%; perpetual license maintenance and software upgrade rights-related arr was $508 million, down 17%; and subscription-based managed services arr was $119 million, up 6%.

Liquidity: Teradata ended 2020 with $529 million in cash and cash equivalents, an increase of $35 million from 2019. Cash provided by operating activities increased by $119 million to $267 million in 2020 compared to 2019. Teradata used approximately $58 million of cash in 2020 for reorganizing and restructuring operations and go-to-market functions. Free cash flow, defined as net cash provided by operating activities less capital expenditures, was $189 million in 2020 and $245 million in 2019. Share repurchases totaled $100 million in 2020 and $300 million in 2019. Total cash and cash equivalents held outside the United States was $342 million as of December 31, 2020 and $344 million as of December 31, 2019. Management believes current cash, cash generated from operations and the $400 million available under the credit facility will be sufficient to satisfy future working capital, research and development activities, capital expenditures, pension contributions, and other financing requirements for at least the next twelve months.

Debt: The company adopted ASU No. 2016-02, "Leases (Topic 842)", which requires leases with durations greater than twelve months to be recognized on the balance sheet. These leases consist of automobiles and real estate, including office, storage and parking space and generally have remaining terms of 2-6 years. Finance leases primarily consist of equipment with terms of 3 years. Operating lease liabilities represent the present value of lease payments not yet paid, and are estimated using the incremental borrowing rate. Leases with an initial term of 12 months or less are not recorded on the balance sheet, and changes in judgments and estimates could impact the amounts of assets or liabilities recorded. As of December 31, 2020, the company's long-term debt and minimum debt obligations are discussed in Note 12 of Notes to Consolidated Financial Statements, and leases and minimum lease obligations are discussed in Note 13.
