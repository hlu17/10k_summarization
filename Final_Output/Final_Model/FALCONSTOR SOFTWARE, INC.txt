Business Overview: Falconstor is a data protection leader that offers enterprise solutions to modernize their data backup and archival operations across sites and public clouds, with 47 patents and over an exabyte of data under management. Their products are software-defined, hardware-agnostic and cloud-compatible, allowing customers to leverage existing investments while providing data security, portability and accessibility. In 2020, they delivered 26% year-over-year new customer bookings growth and achieved $1.1 million of net income in Q1. They also released Storsafe, a next-gen VTL product built for the cloud, and enhanced their NSS and CDP business continuity products. They focus on enterprise customers, managed service providers and OEM partners, offering capacity-based licenses on a subscription or term-based model. Revenues are recognized at a point-in-time for software licenses, ratably over the term of the contract for maintenance services and upon customer acceptance for professional services.

Revenues: Product revenue increased 5% from $6.8 million for the year ended December 31, 2019 to $7.1 million for the year ended December 31, 2020, representing 48% and 41% of total revenue, respectively. Support and services revenue decreased 22% from $9.8 million for the year ended December 31, 2019 to $7.7 million for the year ended December 31, 2020, primarily due to decreases in maintenance and technical support services revenue and professional services revenue from legacy contracts which have termed.

Gross Profit Margin: Gross Profit Margin decreased by 5% from $13.6 million in 2019 to $12.9 million in 2020. Cost of product revenue decreased by 59% from $0.7 million in 2019 to $0.3 million in 2020 and product gross margin increased to 96% from 89%. Cost of support and service revenue decreased by 30% from $2.2 million in 2019 to $1.5 million in 2020 and support and service gross margin increased to 80% from 78%. The increase in total gross margin was primarily due to product mix as a result of an intentional shift away from selling hardware.

Operating Income: Operating Income decreased by $0.7 million, or 23%, to $2.5 million in 2020 from $3.2 million in 2019. Selling and Marketing expenses increased by $0.3 million, or 6%, to $4.6 million in 2020 from $4.3 million in 2019. General and Administrative expenses decreased by $2.6 million, or 46%, to $3.0 million in 2020 from $5.6 million in 2019. Restructuring costs decreased by $0.1 million in 2020 to $1.0 million, compared to a $1.1 million restructuring charge in the prior year period. The decrease in Operating Income, General and Administrative expenses and Restructuring costs was primarily due to the company's realignment and reduction in workforce and expense control plans. The increase in Selling and Marketing expenses was attributable to increased investments made in the Americas and EMEA.

Interest Expense: Interest and other expense increased $0.1 million for the year ended december 31, 2020 to $0.7 million, compared to $0.6 million for the year ended december 31, 2019. interest and other expense is comprised of interest expense on our term loan, foreign currency gains and losses and the change in fair value our embedded derivatives.

Liquidity: Principal sources of liquidity for the company as of December 31, 2020 totaled $1.9 million, compared with $1.5 million as of December 31, 2019. On December 27, 2019, the company entered into an amendment to the amended and restated term loan agreement which provides for a new $2,500,000 term loan facility with an interest rate of 15%. The company drew down $1,000,000 of the 2019 term loan which required a fixed amount of interest on such advance equal to 15% of the principal amount advanced. The company paid the 2019 term loan in full on September 27, 2020. On April 28, 2020, the company entered into a loan with Peapack-Gladstone Bank in an aggregate principal amount of $754,000 pursuant to the Paycheck Protection Program. Net cash provided by operating activities totaled $0.7 million for the year ended December 31, 2020 compared with $1.9 million of net cash used in operating activities in the prior year period. Net cash used in financing activities totaled $0.2 million for the year ended December 31, 2020, compared with net cash provided by financing activities of $0.5 million for the prior year period. As of December 31, 2020, significant commitments relate to the amended and restated term loan credit agreement, operating leases for office facilities, dividends (including accrued dividends) on our series a preferred stock, and the potential redemption of the series a preferred stock.

Debt: As of december 31, 2020 and 2019, we had no off-balance sheet arrangements.
