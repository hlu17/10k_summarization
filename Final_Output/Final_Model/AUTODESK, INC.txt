Results of Operations: In the quarter ending January 31, 2021, usage levels exceeded pre-COVID-19 rates in Asia Pacific and Europe, but remained below in the US and UK. Revenue growth came from record ebas, strong subscription renewals, digital sales, and new business. The AEC and MFG sectors grew compared to the previous year. The company supported customers by extending payment terms, offering free commercial use of cloud products, and delaying license transitions. They managed spending and invested in critical areas like R&D and digitization. The impact of COVID-19 on the business depends on various factors, including the pandemics duration, government actions, and economic recovery.Net revenue consists of subscription, maintenance, and other revenues. For fiscal 2022, the company expects changes in absolute dollar amounts and percent of revenue change. Interest and other expenses increased in 2021 due to impairments and adjustments on strategic investments, offset by a decrease in interest expense and increase in marketable securities gains.Provision for income taxes is accounted for under the liability method. The tax benefit for fiscal 2021 came from the US valuation allowance release, share-based compensation deductions, and federal tax credits, while tax expense for fiscal 2020 was due to foreign tax expense and US tax amortization. The company released the Singapore valuation allowance in fiscal 2020 and the US valuation allowance in fiscal 2021. The future effective annual tax rate may be impacted by various factors, including foreign earnings, valuation allowances, changes in tax laws, and more.

Revenues: Autodesk's total net revenue was $3.79 billion during fiscal 2021, an increase of 16% compared to the prior fiscal year. Recurring revenue as a percentage of net revenue was 97% for the fiscal year ending January 31, 2021, compared to 96% for the same period in the prior fiscal year. The Net Revenue Retention Rate (“NR3”) was within the range of 100% and 110% as of January 31, 2021, and within the range of 110% and 120% as of January 31, 2020. Deferred revenue was $3.36 billion, an increase of 12% compared to the prior fiscal year. Remaining Performance Obligations (short-term and long-term deferred revenue plus unbilled deferred revenue) (“RPO”) was $4.24 billion, an increase of 19% compared to the fourth quarter in the prior fiscal year. Current Remaining Performance Obligations were $2.74 billion, an increase of 16% compared to the prior fiscal year. Subscription revenue increased 26%, while maintenance revenue decreased 53%. Tech Data and Ingram Micro accounted for 37% and 10% of Autodesk's total net revenue, respectively, during fiscal 2021. Cash flow from operations increased to $1,437.2 million for the fiscal year ended January 31, 2021, from $1,415.1 million for the fiscal year ended January 31, 2020. Autodesk repurchased 2.6 million shares of its common stock for $549.4 million during fiscal 2021.

Liquidity: Autodesk has primary sources of liquidity from the sale of software and services, payment of operating costs, acquisition of businesses and technologies, repayment of debt, repurchase of common stock, and capital expenditures. As of January 31, 2021, the company had $1.86 billion in cash, cash equivalents, and marketable securities and $643.1 million in net accounts receivable. Autodesk has a $650.0 million line of credit facility and $1.65 billion in long-term notes payable outstanding. Cash and cash equivalents are held by diversified financial institutions globally, with 59% of the total held outside of the US. Net cash provided by operating activities in fiscal 2021 was $1.44 billion, consisting of net income adjusted for non-cash items and an increase in deferred revenue and accounts payable and other liabilities. Net cash used in investing activities was $403.9 million, primarily due to business combinations, capital expenditures, and purchases of marketable securities. Net cash used in financing activities was $1,046.8 million, primarily due to repurchases of common stock and repayment of debt.
