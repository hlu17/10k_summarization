Business Overview: Cerence builds AI-powered virtual assistants for the mobility/transportation market, primarily targeting the automobile market, with solutions applicable to all forms of transportation. Revenue is generated primarily through the sale of software licenses and cloud-connected services. Total revenue grew 17.0% and 9.1% in 2021 and 2020 respectively, driven by increased market penetration of connected and professional services solutions. Total cost of revenues decreased 6.3% in 2021, primarily due to cost savings initiatives, while total operating expenses grew 12.4%, driven by innovation initiatives. R&D expenses increased 26.1% in 2021 as the R&D and engineering workforces refocused on developing new products and advancing core technologies.

Results of Operations: Total revenues in fiscal year 2021 reached $387.2 million, up 17.0% from $331.0 million in fiscal year 2020. The increase occurred across all product types, with license revenue rising 23.1% to $202.2 million, connected services revenue growing 12.4% to $109.5 million, and professional services revenue increasing 9.0% to $75.5 million. This growth was primarily driven by higher licensing royalties, market penetration of connected services solutions, and demand for integration and customization services. In fiscal year 2020, total revenues reached $331.0 million, up 9.1% from $303.3 million in fiscal year 2019.However, the global automotive industry is currently facing a semiconductor shortage, which may impact the business, including license revenue. Cost of revenues for fiscal year 2021 was $101.1 million, down 6.3% from $107.9 million in fiscal year 2020, mainly due to cost-saving initiatives. Gross profit increased 28.2% from $223.1 million to $286.1 million in the same period. R&D expenses for fiscal year 2021 were $112.1 million, up 26.1% from $88.9 million in fiscal year 2020, mainly due to salary-related expenditures and third-party contractor costs. Sales and marketing expenses reached $38.7 million, a 15.8% increase from $33.4 million in fiscal year 2020, while general and administrative expenses were $57.0 million, up 15.4% from $49.4 million in fiscal year 2020.

Operating Income: Operating Income: Revenue consists primarily of license revenue, connected services revenue and professional services revenue. Cost of revenue includes third-party royalty expenses, labor costs, infrastructure and communication fees, and compensation for services personnel. Operating expenses include research and development, sales and marketing, general and administrative, amortization of acquired patents and core technology, and restructuring and other costs. Acquisition-related costs include transition and integration costs, professional service fees, and fair value adjustments. Total other expense consists primarily of foreign exchange gains (losses), losses on the extinguishment of debt and interest expense.

Liquidity: Liquidity: Our principal sources of liquidity are our cash, cash equivalents, and marketable securities, as well as the cash flows we generated from our operations. We have $166.2 million of cash, cash equivalents and marketable securities as of September 30, 2021. We may finance our liquidity needs through the issuance of equity securities or debt offerings. The COVID-19 pandemic has negatively impacted the global economy, creating significant volatility and disruption of financial markets. An extended period of economic disruption could materially affect our business, results of operations, ability to meet debt covenants, access to sources of liquidity and financial condition.

Debt: Company has entered into facility and equipment leases which qualify as operating and finance leases, with remaining terms ranging from less than one year to seven years. Company has also entered into a credit agreement with a four-year senior secured term loan facility in the aggregate principal amount of $125.0 million and a senior secured first-lien revolving credit facility in an aggregate principal amount of $50.0 million. Obligations under the credit agreement are secured by substantially all of Companys tangible and intangible personal property and material real property and are guaranteed by certain of Companys existing and future direct and indirect wholly owned domestic subsidiaries. Amendment No. 1 to the credit agreement extended the scheduled maturity date of the revolving credit and term facilities from June 12, 2024 to April 1, 2025 and revised certain interest rates. Total interest expense relating to the senior credit facilities for the fiscal year ended September 30, 2021 and 2020 were $4.1 million and $1.5 million, respectively. The amendment also revised the amount by which Company is obligated to make quarterly principal payments. The credit agreement contains certain affirmative and negative covenants and financial covenants, each tested quarterly.
