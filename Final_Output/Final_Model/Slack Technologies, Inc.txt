Business Overview: Slack is a business technology platform that enables users to work together more effectively, connect with other software tools and services, and access information needed to do their best work. It is used across various industries and in over 150 countries, and is available in 10 languages. Revenue for the years ended January 31, 2021, 2020 and 2019 was $902.6 million, $630.4 million, and $400.6 million respectively, representing a 43% and 57% annual growth. Net losses for the same period were $300.4 million, $571.1 million, and $140.7 million, including $227.4 million, $426.5 million, and $23.1 million of stock-based compensation. Slack offers four subscription plans and has 850,000 third-party applications. It has a go-to-market approach combining self-service and direct sales, and focuses on customer experience and customer success.

Results of Operations: Revenue for the year ended January 31, 2020 increased by $229.9 million (57%) compared to the prior year. This was driven by expansion within existing customers, growth in number of paid customers (25%), and increased revenue from customers >$100,000 (46%). Cost of revenue increased by $45.9 million (89%) due to stock-based compensation, third-party hosting costs, personnel costs, and facility- and IT-related overhead costs. Research and development expenses increased by $299.8 million (190%), primarily due to stock-based compensation, personnel costs, and facility- and IT-related overhead costs. Sales and marketing expenses increased by $169.6 million (73%), primarily due to stock-based compensation, personnel costs, third-party hosting costs, and travel-related costs. General and administrative expenses increased by $148.6 million (132%), primarily due to stock-based compensation, one-time fees related to direct listing, personnel costs, and facility- and IT-related overhead costs. Interest expense increased by $0.4 million, while interest income and other income, net increased by $4.8 million due to increased interest income and net realized and unrealized gains from strategic investments.

Revenues: Revenues: Substantially all of our revenue is generated through sales of subscriptions of Slack to organizations. Revenue increased by $272.2 million, or 43%, for the year ended january 31, 2021 compared to the year ended january 31, 2020. The increase was primarily due to expansion within our existing paid customers and the addition of new paid customers, as well as revenue generated from paid customers over $100,000. Cost of Revenue: Cost of revenue increased by $24.5 million, or 25%, for the year ended january 31, 2021 compared to the year ended january 31, 2020. This increase was primarily driven by an increase in third-party hosting costs due to the number of organizations on and usage of Slack, an increase in personnel costs, and an increase in credit card payment processing fees. Operating Expenses: Research and development expenses decreased by $75.2 million, or 16%, for the year ended january 31, 2021 compared to the year ended january 31, 2020. Sales and marketing expenses increased by $57.9 million, or 14%, for the year ended january 31, 2021 compared to the year ended january 31, 2020. General and administrative expenses decreased by $40.2 million, or 15%, for the year ended january 31, 2021 compared to the year ended january 31, 2020. Interest expense increased by $37.2 million for the year ended january 31, 2021 compared to the year ended january 31, 2020. Interest income and other income, net was $28.4 million for the year ended january 31, 2021, an increase of $7.0 million from the year ended january 31, 2020. Provision for (benefit from) income taxes was a benefit of $0.3 million for the year ended january 31, 2021, compared to a provision for income taxes of $0.6 million for the year ended january 31, 2020.

Gross Profit Margin: cost of revenue consists primarily of expenses related to hosting slack and providing ongoing customer support for paid customers. these expenses include employee compensation (including stock-based compensation) and other employee-related expenses for customer experience, professional services, and technical operations staff, payments to outside service providers, third-party hosting costs, payment processing fees, and amortization expense associated with internally-developed and purchased technology . we expect our cost of revenue to continue to increase in absolute dollar amounts as we grow our business and revenue.

Operating Income: Operating Income: Research and Development expenses consist primarily of personnel costs and allocated overhead. Sales and Marketing expenses consist primarily of personnel costs, expenses associated with our marketing and business development programs, and customer experience and technical operations employee overhead costs. General and Administrative expenses consist primarily of personnel costs for finance and accounting, legal, human resources, and other administrative teams as well as for certain executives and professional fees. We plan to increase the dollar amount of our investment in research and development, sales and marketing, and general and administrative for the foreseeable future, and expect our expenses to decrease as a percentage of our revenue over time as our revenue grows. We use non-GAAP financial information to evaluate our ongoing operations and for internal planning and forecasting purposes. We measure our performance using key business metrics such as the number of paid customers.

Interest Expense: in april 2020, we completed the private offering of the notes, issuing $862.5 million aggregate principal amount of 0.50% convertible senior notes due april 15, 2025. after our issuance of the notes, interest expense consists primarily of contractual interest expense and amortization of the discount and debt issuance costs on the notes.interest income and other income, net consists primarily of interest income earned on our cash, cash equivalents, and marketable securities, the change in fair value of our strategic investments, and gains or losses on foreign currency exchange.

Liquidity: Our principal sources of liquidity as of January 31, 2021 were cash and cash equivalents of $1.1 billion and marketable securities of $505.9 million. We have financed our operations primarily through proceeds from the issuance of our convertible preferred stock, the notes, common stock, and cash generated from the sale of our subscriptions. We expect to continue to incur operating losses for the foreseeable future due to the investments that we intend to make in our business and, as a result, we may require additional capital resources to grow our business. We have entered into a $215.0 million revolving credit and guaranty agreement with a syndicate of financial institutions, and in April 2020, we completed our private offering of the notes and received aggregate proceeds of $862.5 million. We believe that current cash, cash equivalents, marketable securities, and available borrowing capacity under the revolving credit facility will be sufficient to fund our operations for at least the next 12 months. Our largest source of operating cash is cash collections from organizations on a paid subscription plan. Free cash flow for the year ended January 31, 2021 was $9.3 million, and net cash provided by financing activities for the year ended January 31, 2021 was $773.0 million, primarily driven by proceeds from the issuance of the notes of $841.3 million, net of issuance costs. Net cash used in investing activities during the year ended January 31, 2021 was $263.1 million, which was primarily driven by purchases of marketable securities of $561.2 million.

Debt: We had no relationships with unconsolidated entities or financial partnerships as of January 31, 2021. Our principal contractual commitments consist of convertible senior notes and obligations under leases for office space and datacenter operations. We had $862.5 million in principal payments, $38.5 million in standby letters of credit, and $425.0 million in minimum annual commitments under an amended agreement with Amazon Web Services, all due by April 2025. We have minimum payments under non-cancelable purchase commitments related to IT operations, sales activities and acquisition related obligations. The notes are accounted for in accordance with ASC subtopic 470-20, and the issuance costs related to the notes are allocated between the liability and equity components based on their relative values.
