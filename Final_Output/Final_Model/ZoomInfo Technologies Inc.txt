Business Overview: Zoominfo is a go-to-market intelligence platform for sales and marketing professionals. It provides highly accurate and comprehensive data and insights on organizations and professionals, enabling sellers and marketers to shorten sales cycles and increase win rates. The platform is powered by an AI- and ML-powered engine that gathers data from millions of sources. It has been re-branded as Zoominfo in 2019 after acquiring pre-acquisition Zi. Zoominfo offers its platform on a subscription basis to both new and existing customers. It generated revenue of $476.2 million in 2020, with an income from operations of $37.1 million. Its adjusted operating income was $226.0 million, with an adjusted operating income margin of 47%. It serves over 20,000 companies in almost every industry vertical, with 45% and 23% of its customers in the software and business services industries respectively. Its net annual retention rate was 108%, 109%, and 102% in 2020, 2019, and 2018, respectively.

Results of Operations: Revenue for the year ended December 31, 2020, reached $476.2 million, a 62% increase compared to $293.3 million in 2019. This growth was mainly due to new customer acquisition and expansion with existing customers, as well as the recognition of revenue for renewed contracts at contracted value. The cost of service for 2020 increased by 57% to $107.5 million compared to $68.7 million in 2019. Operating expenses rose by 76% to $331.6 million in 2020, primarily due to additional stock-based compensation expenses related to grants modified in December 2019 and triggered by the IPO.Net interest expense decreased by 32% to $69.3 million in 2020, while loss on debt extinguishment reached $14.9 million, associated with penalties and derecognition of deferred and unamortized debt issuance costs from repaying term loans after the IPO. Other (income) expense, net, declined by $15.4 million compared to 2019, and the income tax expense for 2020 was $(4.7) million (a (14.8)% effective tax rate) compared to $6.5 million (a 7.7% effective tax rate) in 2019.In comparison, revenue for the year ended December 31, 2019, increased by 103% compared to 2018, mainly due to the acquisitions of pre-acquisition zi, neverbounce, and komiko, as well as organic customer growth and net expansion with existing customers. The cost of service and operating expenses also increased in 2019, and the income tax benefit was $6.5 million (a 7.7% effective tax rate) compared to $2.9 million (a 9.1% effective tax rate) in 2018.

Gross Profit Margin: gross profit is revenue less cost of service, and gross margin is gross profit as a percentage of revenue. gross profit has been and will continue to be affected by various factors, including leveraging economies of scale, the costs associated with third-party hosting services and third-party data, the level of amortization of acquired technology, and the extent to which we expand our customer support and research organizations. we expect that our gross margin will fluctuate from period to period depending on the interplay of these various factors.

Operating Income: Operating expenses comprise sales and marketing, research and development, general and administrative, restructuring and transaction expenses, and amortization of acquired intangibles. Personnel costs, including salaries, bonuses, sales commissions, stock-based compensation, and employee benefits, constitute the largest portion of operating expenses. Other expenses include overhead costs, technology, professional fees, depreciation, and marketing.Sales and marketing expenses primarily cover employee compensation, overhead costs, technology, and marketing programs. The company anticipates a decrease in sales and marketing expenses as a percentage of revenue as equity-based compensation expense becomes less significant.Research and development expenses support the enhancement of the existing platform and the development of new software products. The company expects research and development expenses to increase as it invests in resources to strengthen and enhance its solutions.General and administrative expenses consist of employee-related costs and professional fees, including legal services, accounting, and consulting services. These expenses are expected to remain consistent or decrease modestly from 2020.Amortization of acquired intangibles includes customer relationships, trade names, and brand portfolios. Restructuring and transaction expenses cover various restructuring and acquisition activities to achieve strategic or financial objectives.

Interest Expense: interest expense represents the interest payable on our debt obligations and the amortization of debt discounts and debt issuance costs, less interest income. we anticipate that our interest expense will be substantially lower after having repaid a portion of our outstanding indebtedness with the proceeds from the ipo. interest expense could increase in the future based on changes in variable interest rates or the issuance of additional debt.

Liquidity: As of December 31, 2020, the company had $269.8 million in cash and cash equivalents, $30.6 million in short-term investments, $1.2 million in restricted cash, and $100 million available under its first lien revolving credit facility, which was increased to $250 million in February 2021. The company operations are primarily financed through cash generated from operations and debt offerings.The company believes that its cash flows from operations, existing cash and cash equivalents, and other available external financing sources will be sufficient to fund operating and capital needs for at least the next 12 months.
