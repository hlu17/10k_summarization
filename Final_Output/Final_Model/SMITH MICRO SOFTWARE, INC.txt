Business Overview: Smith Micro applies provisions of FASB ASC Topic No. 805, Business Combinations, in accounting for acquisitions, which requires recognition of assets and liabilities acquired at their acquisition date fair values. It provides software solutions to leading wireless and cable service providers to simplify and enhance the mobile experience. Its portfolio includes products for creating, sharing, and monetizing rich content. In 2020, wireless revenues increased due to higher subscriber numbers, but were negatively impacted by the closure of customer retail stores due to the COVID-19 pandemic and the T-Mobile/Sprint merger. Commsuite revenues were relatively stable. The company received additional cash proceeds of $4.2 million from the exercise of warrants issued in 2018.

Results of Operations: Revenues from sales to T-Mobile (including Sprint) accounted for 81-84% of total revenues and 91-92% of accounts receivable for 2020 and 2019. Total revenues for 2020 increased 18% to $51.3 million, driven by higher Safepath subscriber numbers and additional revenues from the Circle acquisition. Cost of revenues increased 32% to $5.2 million, due to hosting costs associated with the Safepath revenues and additional costs associated with the new Circle operator platform. Gross profit was 90% of revenues in 2020, an increase of 17% from 91% of revenues in 2019. Selling and marketing expenses increased 42% to $10.7 million, due to additional headcount-related expenses and increased intangibles amortization related to the Circle acquisition. Research and development expenses increased 63% to $19.2 million, due to additional headcount related expenses, external contract development costs to support Safepath development, and increased intangibles amortization related to the Circle acquisition. General and administrative expenses increased 29% to $12.8 million, due to an increase in non-cash stock compensation, variable compensation expense, and fees associated with the Circle acquisition. Restructuring expenses were $19 thousand in 2020 and $194 thousand in 2019. The gain on sale of software products was $0.7 million in 2020 and $0.5 million in 2019. Interest income was $96 thousand and $228 thousand in 2020 and 2019, respectively. The current provision for income tax expense consists of state income tax minimums, foreign tax withholdings, and foreign income taxes; a valuation allowance related to deferred tax assets of $49.4 million was retained at December 31, 2020.

Operating Income: In 2020, net cash provided by operating activities was $7.9 million, primarily due to net income of $4.2 million and add-backs of non-cash expenses totaling $7.2 million, offset by an increase in accounts receivable of $1.3 million and a decrease in accounts payable and accrued liabilities of $1.9 million. in 2019, net cash provided by operating activities was $10.0 million, primarily due to net income of $10.7 million.

Liquidity: At December 31, 2020, cash and cash equivalents was $25.8 million and working capital was $30.9 million. In 2020, cash provided by financing activities was $4.2 million due to proceeds from the exercise of common stock warrants. In 2019, cash provided by financing activities was $11.4 million, mainly from the exercise of common stock warrants, offset by $0.1 million in preferred stock dividend payments. In 2020, cash used in investing activities was $14.7 million due to payments related to the circle acquisition, capital expenditures and an equity investment, offset by proceeds from the sale of animation software. In 2019, cash used in investing activities was $5.3 million due to payments related to the smart retail acquisition and capital expenditures, offset by proceeds from the sale of animation software.
