Business Overview:We offer the market-leading software intelligence platform for dynamic multicloud environments to help enterprises embrace digital transformation. Our all-in-one platform provides observability to enable organizations to innovate faster, simplify cloud complexity and secure cloud-native applications. As of March 31, 2021, our products are used by approximately 2,900 customers in 90 countries. Our revenue is generated mainly through software-as-a-service (“SaaS”) agreements, term-based licenses, perpetual licenses and maintenance and support agreements. We target the largest 15,000 global enterprise accounts and measure our performance through key metrics such as customers, annual recurring revenue (“ARR”) and total revenue.

Results of Operations: For the year ended March 31, 2021, subscription revenue increased by $167.4 million (34%) and subscription gross profit increased by $163.1 million (39%) compared to the previous year. License revenue decreased by $11.2 million (89%) while service revenue increased by $1.6 million (3%). Research and development expenses decreased by $7.9 million (7%), sales and marketing expenses decreased by $20.7 million (8%), and general and administrative expenses decreased by $69.8 million (43%). Amortization of acquired technologies decreased by $5.5 million (14%), and other expense, net decreased by $32.6 million (70%).For the year ended March 31, 2020, subscription revenue increased by $138.0 million (39%) and subscription gross profit increased by $121.7 million (42%) compared to the year ended March 31, 2019. License revenue decreased by $27.7 million (69%) and service revenue increased by $4.5 million (11%). Research and development expenses increased by $42.5 million (55%), sales and marketing expenses increased by $87.3 million (49%), and general and administrative expenses increased by $70.2 million (76%). Amortization of other intangibles decreased by $7.4 million (16%), and other expense, net decreased by $20.6 million (31%).

Revenues: subscription revenue: consists of saas agreements, term-based licenses, perpetual licenses and maintenance and support agreements; recognized ratably over the term of the applicable agreement; expected to increase as a percentage of total revenue. license revenue: reflects revenue from sales of perpetual and term-based licenses; recognized upfront assuming all revenue recognition criteria are satisfied. service revenue: consists of revenue from helping customers deploy and train personnel; recognized on a time and materials basis as services are delivered. arr: defined as daily revenue of all subscription agreements multiplied by 365; grew $201 million, or 35%, over the past year.

Gross Profit Margin: Gross Profit Margin is calculated by subtracting cost of revenue from revenue. Cost of revenue includes direct costs associated with subscription and service products, such as salaries, benefits, share-based compensation and related expenses, allocated overhead and amortization of internally developed and acquired technology. Gross margin is expected to fluctuate depending on the mix of license, subscription and services revenue, costs associated with cloud-based hosting services and the extent of customer support and services organizations.

Operating Income: Operating expenses consist of personnel costs, sales and marketing expenses, research and development expenses, general and administrative expenses, amortization of other intangibles, and restructuring and other expenses. Cash provided by operating activities in 2021 was $220.4 million, in 2020 was $142.5 million, and in 2019 was $147.1 million. Non-cash charges are primarily comprised of depreciation and amortization, share-based compensation, and deferred income taxes. Changes in operating assets and liabilities are primarily due to seasonality in sales cycle, timing of payments, and timing of receipts of payments from customers.
