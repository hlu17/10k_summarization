Business Overview: The company generates revenue through the sale of enterprise video content management software, hardware, maintenance and support, and professional and other services. software sales may take the form of a perpetual software license, a cloud-hosted software as a service (saas) or a term software license. software licenses and appliances revenue includes sales of perpetual software licenses and hardware. service revenue includes saas, term software licenses, maintenance and support, and professional and other services. for the years ended december 31, 2020, 2019 and 2018, the company generated revenues of $29.1 million, $25.4 million and $25.0 million, respectively.

Revenues: Qumu's revenues increased from 2019 to 2020 due to a large customer order, driven by the change in working environment due to COVID-19. The increase was primarily driven by increased software licenses and appliances revenues and service revenues. Service revenue included subscription, maintenance and support, and professional services. Factors influencing future consolidated revenues include the rate of adoption of the company's software solutions, customer orders and renewals, product and service mix, economic conditions, and foreign currency exchange rate fluctuations.

Gross Profit Margin: .5% decrease in gross profit margin in 2020 compared to 2019; 6.2% improvement in gross profit margin in 2019 compared to 2018; 0.9% decrease in service gross margin in 2020 compared to 2019; 7.2% improvement in service gross margin in 2019 compared to 2018; $0.1 million of amortization expense for purchased intangibles expected in 2021.

Operating Income: Operating expenses increased 19% for 2020 compared to 2019, representing 96.4%, 93.1%, and 93.6% of revenues for 2020, 2019 and 2018, respectively. The increase was primarily driven by one-time transaction expense associated with the company’s terminated merger and non-cash office lease charges resulting from remote work policy adoption. Severance expense for 2020, 2019 and 2018 related to cost reduction initiatives and personnel transitions was $647,000, $152,000 and $237,000 respectively. Research and Development expenses for 2020, 2019 and 2018 represented 28%, 29% and 28% of revenues, respectively, with an increase in 2020 primarily due to personnel costs and projects to support customers’ increased usage of qumu’s cloud-based enterprise video solution. Sales and Marketing expenses for 2020, 2019 and 2018 represented 31%, 34% and 34% of revenues, respectively, with an increase in 2020 due to outside services and consulting, compensation costs and commissions expense. General and Administrative expenses for 2020, 2019 and 2018 represented 35%, 27% and 29% of revenues, respectively, with an increase in 2020 due to transaction-related expenses, non-cash charges and severance costs. Amortization of purchased intangibles for 2020, 2019 and 2018 was $657,000, $757,000 and $904,000 respectively. 2021 is expected to include approximately $0.7 million of amortization expense.

Liquidity: The company's liquidity and capital resources include $23.1 million in net proceeds from the issuance of common stock and a $10.0 million revolving credit facility, both of which closed in January 2021. As of December 31, 2020, the company had aggregate negative working capital of $2.9 million and financing obligations primarily consisted of finance leases related to the acquisition of computer and network equipment. Net cash provided from operating activities was $1.6 million in 2020, compared to net cash used in operating activities of $1.5 million in 2019. Net cash used in investing activities for the purchases of property and equipment totaled $128,000, $168,000 and $127,000 in 2020, 2019 and 2018, respectively. Financing activities used net cash of $120,000 in 2020 and provided net cash of $3.6 million in 2019. The company did not repurchase any shares of its common stock under the repurchase program during the years ended December 31, 2020, 2019 and 2018. The company did not declare or pay any dividends during the years ended December 31, 2020, 2019 and 2018.

Debt: The Company has non-cancellable leases for office space and IT equipment expiring over the next three years. Short-term leases of 12 months or less are recognized as an expense on a straight-line basis. Some leases have escalation, renewal, or termination options that affect lease payments. In December 2020, the Company transitioned to remote work and closed three offices, resulting in a $433,000 reduction of lease terms and operating lease liabilities. The Company also incurred non-cash expenses of $917,000 related to ROU assets and the surrender of leasehold improvements. The Company entered into short-term flexible shared workspace arrangements. Two warrants for the purchase of up to 1,239,286 shares of the Companys common stock were issued in 2016 and 2018. One warrant representing 314,286 shares was outstanding as of December 31, 2020, and is recorded as a current liability at fair value on issuance date and revalued on each balance sheet date until expiration or exercise.
